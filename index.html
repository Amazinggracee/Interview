  2. What is your greatest strength?


    1. Curiosity
      It pushes me to learn new languages, experiment, and look for new solutions.
    2. Technical skills
      Fashion art and programming are similar in the sense that you have to work with a design and with a very good color combination that will meet the client's test. Greatness doesn’t come from technical skills alone. However, you can’t expect exceptional results from people who don’t have the right skills for the job. You should think of skills as one of the elements of the puzzle which doesn’t do much in isolation. It can do wonders when accompanied by other developer strengths
    3. Love of learning
      You have to love the fundamental practice of going from not knowing to knowing, every single day” otherwise you won’t be good at it. This holds especially true in the world of IT. While it’s useful to have your way of doing things, it’s essential you venture out of what you know. Find faster ways of getting things done. One of the ways to do that is by noticing patterns and drawing conclusions.
    4. A positive attitude
      One of the qualities of a good programmer is that they solve problems day in and day out, but that doesn’t mean they should dwell on them. When tasks and tickets tend to pile up I try to wear a good attitude. And I always have this in mind that everyone is facing one challenge or the other, we all need this challenge to grow.
    5. Embracing mentorship
      In my country we have this slang "follow who knows the road". It simply takes less time to figure things out if you can watch a more seasoned colleague at work. Another reason why top techies advocate mentorship is because it requires you to expose both your strengths and weaknesses. This teaches you volumes about keeping your ego in check.
    6. Communication skills
      • You need to know what questions to ask when you don’t understand or need to double-check
      • It’s possible to figure things out based on communication with colleagues. That means you don’t need to rely on written specifications.
      • Concepts are understood and communicated quickly in a team of successful developers.
      • One of the qualities of a good programmer is that they can communicate cogently with both technical and non-technical staff.

  3. What is your greatest weakness?

    1. I have a hard time letting go of a project
      I spent a great deal of time and effort on something, it’s easy to feel apprehensive about marking it complete or passing it to another team. There’s always room for improvement and I tend to over-criticize my work or attempt last-minute changes, which can threaten the timeline.
    2. I sometimes lack confidence
      Lack of confidence is one of my weakness, especially as an entry-level contributors. However, having a lack of confidence can sometimes cause inefficiencies in my work. Though , I have this strong believe that the more projects I venture into the more confident I will be become.
    3. I focus too much on the details
      Being detail-oriented is typically a good thing, but if you’re someone who tends to spend too much time on the specifics of a project like me, it could also be considered a weakness. Is one of the habits I developed from also being a fashion designer. You have to get it right.

    I was managing a project for one of our client who has already booked her flight for summer vacation , and I was so eager to please her that I told her we could finish the project within 2 weeks. I thought this was doable, but it ended up taking three weeks and she was not happy. Looking back, I realized I should have been more conservative in my estimate to the client. I realized that a client isn’t going to be upset if you’re clear about the timeline in advance, but they are going to be disappointed if you promise something and then don’t deliver.
